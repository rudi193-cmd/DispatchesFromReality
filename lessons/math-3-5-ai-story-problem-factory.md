# The AI Story Problem Factory

**Grade Level:** 3–5
**Subject:** Math
**Language:** English
**AI Tool Used:** ChatGPT, Claude, or LevelShip
**Duration:** 45 minutes
**Contributor:** Emerging Rule Team

---

## Objective

Students will be able to write and solve multi-step word problems by using an AI tool as a "story problem generator," then critically evaluate whether the AI's math is correct.

---

## Standards Alignment

- CCSS.MATH.CONTENT.3.OA.D.8 — Solve two-step word problems
- CCSS.MATH.CONTENT.4.OA.A.3 — Multi-step problems with remainders

---

## Materials

- Device with access to an AI chatbot (1 per student or pair)
- Math journal or worksheet
- Printed "AI Fact-Check" rubric (included below)

---

## Lesson Plan

### Warm-Up (5 min)
Ask students: *"Can a computer make math mistakes?"* Take a quick vote. Tell them today they're going to find out.

### Main Activity (30 min)

**Part 1 — Generate (10 min)**
Students type this prompt into the AI:

> **Sample prompt:** "Write a two-step word problem for a 4th grader using multiplication and subtraction. Use a topic I pick: [pizza / baseball / animals / video games]."

Students copy the problem into their math journal.

**Part 2 — Solve (10 min)**
Students solve the problem on their own, showing all work. They do NOT look at the AI's answer yet.

**Part 3 — Fact-Check the AI (10 min)**
Students ask the AI to solve the problem and show its work:

> **Sample prompt:** "Now solve the problem step by step and explain your thinking."

Students compare the AI's solution to their own. Did the AI get it right? Did it explain it clearly?

### Wrap-Up / Discussion (10 min)
- Who found a mistake in the AI's math?
- What does that tell us about using AI as a tool?
- Can you write a harder problem that would trick the AI?

---

## Assessment

**AI Fact-Check Rubric** (students self-assess):
| | Yes | No |
|---|---|---|
| I solved the problem myself before checking the AI | ☐ | ☐ |
| I showed all my work | ☐ | ☐ |
| I found at least one difference between my work and the AI's | ☐ | ☐ |
| I can explain why the AI was right or wrong | ☐ | ☐ |

---

## Differentiation

- **Support:** Provide a simpler prompt template; allow calculator use
- **Extension:** Students write their own problem first, then ask AI to solve it and evaluate the solution
- **ELL/Bilingual:** Allow students to ask the AI in Spanish — compare the problems generated in both languages

---

## Notes from the Classroom

This lesson works great in pairs. The moment a student catches the AI making a math error is genuinely exciting — it shifts them from passive users to critical thinkers. Budget extra time for the discussion; students have a lot to say.
