# Mi Robot de Cuentos / My Story Robot

**Grade Level:** K–2
**Subject:** ELA / Literacy
**Language:** Bilingual (English & Spanish)
**AI Tool Used:** ChatGPT or Claude
**Duration:** 30–40 minutes
**Contributor:** Emerging Rule Team

---

## Objective

Students will use an AI tool as a "story robot" to co-write a short illustrated story, building vocabulary in both English and Spanish while learning that AI needs human creativity to make good stories.

---

## Standards Alignment

- CCSS.ELA-LITERACY.W.K.3 — Use drawing, dictating, and writing to narrate
- CCSS.ELA-LITERACY.SL.1.4 — Describe people, places, things, and events

---

## Materials

- Device with AI chatbot access (teacher-led or 1 per pair)
- Drawing paper and crayons
- Bilingual word wall (optional)

---

## Lesson Plan

### Warm-Up / Calentamiento (5 min)

Ask in both languages:
- *"¿Alguna vez le has pedido ayuda a una computadora?"*
- *"Have you ever asked a computer for help?"*

Tell students: *"Today we have a story robot. But the robot needs YOUR ideas to make the story good!"* / *"¡Hoy tenemos un robot de cuentos. Pero el robot necesita TUS ideas para hacer un buen cuento!"*

### Main Activity (20–25 min)

**Step 1 — Choose your story ingredients (5 min)**
As a class, pick:
- A main character (un personaje): a child, an animal, a monster...
- A place (un lugar): the moon, the forest, a taco stand...
- A problem (un problema): lost, scared, hungry...

**Step 2 — Ask the Story Robot (10 min)**
Teacher types (or older students type):

> **Sample prompt (English):** "Write a very short story for kindergartners about [character] who is [problem] at [place]. Use simple words. Make it 4 sentences."

> **Prompt en español:** "Escribe un cuento muy corto para niños de kínder sobre [personaje] que está [problema] en [lugar]. Usa palabras simples. Hazlo de 4 oraciones."

Read the story aloud together in both languages.

**Step 3 — Improve the story (10 min)**
Ask students: *"What's missing? What should happen next?"* Then ask the AI to add their idea:

> **Sample prompt:** "Now add one more sentence where the character [student's idea]."

**Step 4 — Illustrate (remaining time)**
Each student draws their favorite part of the story.

### Wrap-Up / Cierre (5 min)
- What did YOU add that the robot couldn't think of?
- *¿Qué añadiste TÚ que el robot no pudo pensar?*

---

## Assessment

Observe students during illustration and discussion:
- Can they retell one part of the story?
- Did they contribute an idea that changed the story?

---

## Differentiation

- **Support:** Teacher scribes; students illustrate and dictate
- **Extension:** Students write their own 2-sentence ending independently
- **ELL:** This lesson is designed for bilingual classrooms — run entirely in Spanish if preferred, or toggle between languages for each step

---

## Notes from the Classroom

Young students are delighted when the AI uses the exact character name they chose. Let them name the character themselves — it creates instant ownership. The bilingual prompt comparison (same story in English vs. Spanish) sparks great discussion about language differences.
