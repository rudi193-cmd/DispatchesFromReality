# El Jardín de Números / The Number Garden

**Grade Level:** K–3
**Subject:** Math — Number Sense & Counting
**Language:** Bilingual (Spanish primary / English secondary)
**AI Tool Used:** LevelShip or ChatGPT
**Duration:** 35–40 minutes
**Contributor:** Emerging Rule Team

---

## Objetivo / Objective

Los estudiantes contarán, compararán y ordenarán números del 1 al 20 usando un "jardín de números" interactivo, con el apoyo de un asistente de IA como tutor de conteo.

Students will count, compare, and order numbers 1–20 using an interactive "number garden," supported by an AI assistant as a counting tutor.

---

## Standards Alignment

- CCSS.MATH.CONTENT.K.CC.A.1 — Count to 100 by ones
- CCSS.MATH.CONTENT.1.NBT.B.3 — Compare two-digit numbers

---

## Materiales / Materials

- Device with AI chatbot access (teacher-projected or 1 per pair)
- Counters or manipulatives (beans, buttons, or blocks work great)
- Number cards 1–20 (printable or handwritten)
- Drawing paper

---

## Plan de Lección / Lesson Plan

### Calentamiento / Warm-Up (5 min)

El maestro dice: *"Hoy nuestro jardín necesita flores. Cada flor tiene un número. ¡Ayúdame a plantarlas en orden!"*

Teacher says: *"Today our garden needs flowers. Each flower has a number. Help me plant them in order!"*

Point to scattered number cards on the board. Students take turns placing them in order 1–10.

### Actividad Principal / Main Activity (25 min)

**Parte 1 — El Robot Conta / The Counting Robot (10 min)**

Teacher types (or students help type):

> **Prompt en español:** "Eres un tutor de matemáticas para niños de kínder. Cuéntame del 1 al 10 en español con una historia de un jardín. Usa palabras simples."

> **English prompt:** "You are a math tutor for kindergartners. Count from 1 to 10 in Spanish with a garden story. Use simple words."

Read together. Students hold up fingers as each number appears.

**Parte 2 — ¡Más y menos! / More and Less! (10 min)**

Place two groups of counters on the table. Ask the AI:

> **Prompt:** "I have 7 flowers and 4 flowers. Which group has more? Explain it like I'm 5 years old. Answer in Spanish and English."

Students predict first, then check the AI's answer. Repeat with new numbers students choose.

**Parte 3 — Dibuja tu jardín / Draw Your Garden (5 min)**

Each student draws a garden with a number of flowers they choose (1–20). They write the number and the Spanish word underneath.

### Cierre / Wrap-Up (5 min)

Each student shares their garden number:
- *"Tengo ___ flores."* / *"I have ___ flowers."*
- Class counts together in Spanish and English.

---

## Evaluación / Assessment

Observe students during Part 2:
- Can they predict which group has more before the AI answers?
- Can they count their garden drawing and say the number in Spanish?

---

## Diferenciación / Differentiation

- **Apoyo / Support:** Use numbers 1–5 only; provide number line
- **Extensión / Extension:** Students write a number sentence (e.g. 7 > 4) and ask the AI to check it
- **Bilingüe:** This lesson is designed for dual-language classrooms. Run entirely in Spanish or fully bilingual — the AI handles both.

---

## Notas del salón / Classroom Notes

The garden metaphor works especially well because students can plant, grow, and harvest — natural vocabulary for counting up and down. The AI's bilingual response often surprises students ("the computer speaks Spanish!") and creates a great moment to talk about how technology can support multiple languages.

Beans or buttons as physical counters make the abstract numbers concrete for younger learners.
