## 1. Post-WWII Industrial And Institutional Legacies

This research vector traces how wartime technical expertise, industrial capacity, and intelligence networks moved into postwar institutional frameworks.

→ **Public packet:** [postwar-institutional-legacies/README.md](postwar-institutional-legacies/README.md)

| Area | Examples |
|------|----------|
| Transfer programs | Operation Paperclip and related postwar technical/intelligence transfer networks |
| Industrial continuity | Defense contractors, aerospace infrastructure, research parks, and institutional lineages |
| Long historical anchors | Earlier institutional patterns, including research atoms reaching back to the Queen Anne era (1702-1714) |
| Method | Entity tracking, lineage mapping, public-source synthesis, cross-era comparison |

**Portfolio value:** Shows the ability to connect deep historical context to present institutional structures without treating modern systems as ahistorical.

**Claim boundary:** This is research synthesis and lineage mapping, not a claim of archival completeness.

## Shared method

Across domains, the pattern is consistent:

1. Build or identify a structured evidence base.
2. Track entities and relationships, not just isolated facts.
3. Separate claims from artifacts.
4. Make uncertainty visible.
5. Translate the research into a usable system, paper, lesson, or portfolio artifact.

That is the through-line connecting the sociotechnical paper, Willow, the education work, and the non-software systems research.
