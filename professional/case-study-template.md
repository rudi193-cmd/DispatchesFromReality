# Case Study Template

Use this structure for future professional writeups. Keep claims tied to evidence. Label prototypes, frameworks, and contributor materials honestly.

---

## [Project Title]

**One-line summary:** [What you built or designed, for whom, under what constraint.]

**Status:** [Shipped | Public artifact | Framework | Contributor material | In progress]

**Evidence:** [Link to repo file, public repo, or demo]

---

### Problem

What real constraint or gap existed? Who felt the pain?

---

### Audience / users

Who was this for? (e.g. teachers, students, contributors, operators, agent users)

---

### Constraints

List the non-negotiables: time, devices, privacy, local-first, verification burden, human-in-the-loop, etc.

---

### What I built / designed

Concrete artifacts: documents, curriculum units, APIs, workflows, governance tools, architecture decisions.

Use bullets. Name files or systems where possible.

---

### Technical judgment

Tradeoffs you made and why. What you chose *not* to do. Reliability, equity, or governance choices that matter for a technical reader.

---

### Evidence

Links to primary sources. Prefer repo-relative paths or known public URLs.

---

### Transferable strengths

3–5 skills a hiring manager can map to a role: product thinking, systems design, writing, research synthesis, AI safety, tooling, documentation, etc.

---

## Writing notes

- Lead with the problem, not the technology.
- Use plain professional prose; avoid inflated claims.
- If outcomes are not externally validated, say so.
- Do not include private credentials, internal-only paths, or unreleased customer details.
